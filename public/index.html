Big Old 2 Game Rule

1. The player who has the lowest card play first.

2. For the International Mode, the lowest card is 3 Diamonds. However for the Taiwanese Mode, the lowest card is 3 Clubs.

3. The picture below is the rank of suits in the International Mode from lowest to highest


4. The pictures below is the rank of suits in the Taiwanese Mode from lowest to highest

5. When in control, a player may play any valid 1 (single), 2 (pair) or 5 card hand. Subsequent players must play a valid hand of the same number of cards which is higher than the previous hand played. This continues until everyone passes, at which point control is gained again and any valid hand may be played.

6. The 5 cards hand rank from the lowest to highest is straight, flush, full house, four cards, straight flush

7. Straight (5 numbers in a row) example:


Comparison of Straights is based on highest number primarily, so 2 Spades Straight is the highest.
A-2-3-4-5 and 2-3-4-5-6 are valid but J-Q-K-A-2 is not valid.

8. Flush (5 cards of same suit) example:


Comparison of Flush is based on highest number primarily, so 2 Spades Flush is the highest.

9. Full House (3 of one number + 1 pair) example:


Comparison of Full House is based on higher 3 cards section.

10. Four Cards (4 of one number + 1 single) example:


Comparison of Four Cards is based on higher 4 cards section.

11. Straight Flush (both straight and flush) example:


Comparison of Straight Flush is based on highest number primarily, so 2 Spades Straight Flush is the highest.
